## Usage-Based Pricing Deep Dive Guide

### Applicability Judgment Matrix

| Usage Dimension | Suitable for Usage-Based | Not Suitable |
|----------------|-------------------------|--------------|
| API Calls | ✓ Naturally suited | — |
| Storage Space | ✓ Clear cost correlation | — |
| Compute Resources | ✓ Cloud-native | — |
| Messages/Events | ✓ Predictable growth | ✗ High volatility hard to forecast |
| Data Analysis Volume | ✓ Large customers pay more | ✗ Small customers feel expensive |
| Collaboration Seats | — | ✗ Users dislike "head tax" limits |

### AI Era Pricing Challenges

| Challenge | Description | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Token Pricing** | Inference costs continuously dropping, fixed token pricing may lose competitiveness | Dynamic pricing or "outcome-based pricing" |
| **Inference Cost Volatility** | Different models, different time periods have large cost variance | Cost-plus model, not fixed unit price |
| **Agent Call Chains** | One user request may trigger multiple model calls | Price by "task completion" not "token count" |

## PLG (Product-Led Growth) Deep Dive

### PLG Core Funnel

```
Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral

Key Metrics:
- PQL (Product Qualified Leads): Product-qualified leads — judge purchase intent through product usage behavior
- Time-to-Value (TTV): Time from signup to experiencing core value
- PQL Conversion Rate: PQL → SQL → Closed Won
```

### Global PLG Maturity Model

| Level | Name | Characteristics | Examples |
|-------|------|----------------|----------|
| **L1 - PLG Curious** | Experimenting with free trial or freemium | Basic self-serve signup, no product-qualified lead scoring | Traditional enterprise transitioning |
| **L2 - PLG Emerging** | Self-serve funnel with basic PQL signals | Free tier with usage limits, basic product analytics | Many Series A/B startups |
| **L3 - PLG Operational** | Full PLG motion with PQL scoring and sales handoff | Product-qualified accounts trigger sales outreach, PLS hybrid | Figma, Notion, Miro |
| **L4 - PLG Native** | Product is the primary growth engine | Viral loops, network effects, self-serve revenue >50% | Canva, Airtable, Webflow |
| **L5 - PLG Dominant** | PLG drives >70% of revenue with enterprise overlay | Enterprise sales only for top-tier, everything else self-serve | Atlassian (pre-enterprise push), Slack |

### International PLG Benchmarks

| Company | PLG Model | Free Tier | Key PLG Metric | Lesson |
|---------|-----------|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **Figma** | Freemium + Collaborative | 3 files free, unlimited collaborators | Collaborative design drives team adoption | Collaboration is the ultimate PLG hook |
| **Canva** | Freemium + Template Marketplace | Free with watermark on premium elements | 150M+ MAU, 85% organic | Templates + ease of use = massive top-of-funnel |
| **Miro** | Freemium + Team | 3 editable boards free | Team adoption within enterprises | Bottom-up within orgs, then top-down expansion |
| **Airtable** | Freemium + Usage-Based | Unlimited bases, 1,200 records/base | Record limit as upgrade trigger | Usage-based limits create natural upgrade moments |
| **Webflow** | Freemium + Site-Based | Free staging, paid hosting | Designer-to-developer handoff | Visual development creates lock-in |

### Regional PLG Strategy Differences

| Region | PLG Adoption | Key Adaptation | Notable Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **US** | Highest PLG maturity | Credit card self-serve, 14-day trials | Figma, Notion, Slack |
| **EU** | Growing, GDPR-aware | Longer trials (30 days), data residency options | Miro, Airtable (EU hosting) |
| **UK** | Similar to US | Mix of self-serve and sales-assisted | Monzo, Revolut Business |
| **APAC (SEA)** | Emerging | Mobile-first PLG, local payment gateways, 30-60 day trials | Canva (Australia-origin) |
| **APAC (India)** | Rapidly growing | Very long free tiers, INR pricing, WhatsApp-based onboarding | Zoho, Freshworks |
| **Middle East** | Nascent | Relationship-driven, free POC common | Few pure PLG examples |
| **LATAM** | Early stage | Localized pricing essential, WhatsApp support | Nubank (B2C PLG pioneer) |

### PLG China Market Adaptation

| Dimension | Global PLG | China PLG Adaptation |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Self-Serve Signup | Credit card self-serve | Must support WeChat Pay/Alipay + Fapiao (invoice) |
| Free Trial | 14-30 days | Recommend 30-60 days (longer decision chain) |
| Decision Model | Individual/small team trial → expand | Must cover both decision maker and user dual track |
| Payment Conversion | Online self-serve upgrade | Often requires sales intervention (PLS model) |

## Product-Led Sales (PLS) Hybrid Model

### PLS Hybrid GTM Model

```
PLG (Product-Led)              SLG (Sales-Led)
     │                              │
     ├── Self-serve signup/trial    ├── Enterprise customization
     ├── In-product upgrade prompts ├── Contract negotiation
     └── Self-serve payment         └── Invoice/procurement process
                  │
                  ▼
          PLS (Product-Led Sales)
     ┌─────────────────────────────┐
     │ Use product usage data to   │
     │ identify sales signals      │
     │ Sales intervenes at key     │
     │ moments                     │
     │ 65% of buyers prefer hybrid │
     └─────────────────────────────┘
```

### PQA (Product Qualified Account) Scoring Model

| Dimension | Weight | Signal Examples |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Product Usage Depth | 30% | Core feature usage frequency, advanced feature activation |
| Team Size | 20% | Team members, department count |
| Usage Growth | 20% | Month-over-month usage growth, new feature adoption speed |
| Firmographics | 15% | Industry, company size, tech stack |
| Intent Signals | 15% | Pricing page visits, upgrade button clicks, support inquiries |

### McKinsey Key Finding

> "Only a minority of PLG companies achieve outstanding performance — PLS is the key differentiator"
> Pure PLG trap: Self-serve model struggles to cover enterprise complex needs
> PLS core: Use product data to drive sales, not replace sales

## JTBD (Jobs-to-Be-Done) Framework

### Core Principle

Users "hire" products to complete a job, not to buy product features.

### JTBD Statement Template

```
When [situation/context]
I want to [motivation/functional job]
So I can [expected outcome/emotional job]

Example:
When I need to report department budget execution to the CFO at month-end
I want to quickly generate a visual report with YoY/MoM/budget variance
So I can complete report preparation in 30 minutes without manually pulling data from 5 systems
```

### Four-Dimensional Analysis

| Dimension | Analysis Question |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Time Urgency** | How often does this task need to be completed? What are the consequences of delay? |
| **Decision Level** | Who makes this decision? Individual / Team / Organization? |
| **Input Barriers** | What inputs are needed to complete this task? How difficult to obtain? |
| **Output Requirements** | What format does the task output need? What precision level? |

### Task Card Template

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **JTBD ID** | JOB-001 |
| **Task Description** | [One-sentence description of the task the user needs to complete] |
| **Trigger Condition** | [Under what circumstances does the user need to complete this task] |
| **Success Criteria** | [What counts as done well] |
| **Current Solution** | [How the user does it now] |
| **Pain Points** | [Shortcomings of current solution] |
| **Ideal Experience** | [The ideal state the user expects] |

## Customer Success Metrics System

### Core Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Value | Warning Value |
|--------|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **NRR** (Net Revenue Retention) | (Beginning ARR + Expansion - Churn - Contraction) / Beginning ARR | >120% | <100% |
| **GRR** (Gross Revenue Retention) | (Beginning ARR - Churn) / Beginning ARR | >90% | <80% |
| **Churn Rate** | Churned customers / Beginning customers | <5% annualized | >10% annualized |
| **Expansion Rate** | Expansion ARR / Beginning ARR | >20% | <10% |
| **LTV:CAC** | Customer Lifetime Value / Acquisition Cost | >3:1 | <1:1 |

### Global CS Maturity Model

| Level | Name | Characteristics | CSM:Customer Ratio | Key Metrics |
|-------|------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **L1 - Reactive** | Firefighting mode, respond to tickets | No dedicated CSM | 1:200+ | Ticket volume, response time |
| **L2 - Proactive** | Scheduled check-ins, health scoring | 1:50-100 | 1:50-100 | Health score, NPS |
| **L3 - Prescriptive** | Data-driven interventions, playbooks | 1:20-50 | 1:20-50 | NRR, Expansion Rate |
| **L4 - Strategic** | Business outcome alignment, executive sponsorship | 1:5-20 | 1:5-20 | Customer ROI, Strategic adoption |
| **L5 - Embedded** | CS is company-wide culture, product-led CS | 1:5-10 (enterprise only) | 1:5-10 | Customer Lifetime Value, Advocacy Rate |

### International CS Benchmarks

| Company | CS Approach | NRR | Key Practice |
|---------|------------|-----|-------------|
| **Salesforce** | Strategic CS + Trailblazer Community | ~120% | Customer success platform + community-driven learning |
| **ServiceNow** | Prescriptive CS with impact model | ~125% | Value realization tracking, executive business reviews |
| **Atlassian** | Product-led CS + Community | ~120% | Self-serve knowledge base, community forums, marketplace |
| **HubSpot** | Tiered CS (digital → high-touch) | ~110% | Academy + certifications + partner ecosystem |

### Customer Health Score (Health Score)

| Dimension | Weight | Metrics |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Product Usage | 40% | Login frequency, core feature usage rate, feature adoption depth |
| Business Relationship | 30% | Contract expiration, payment timeliness, expansion history |
| Satisfaction | 20% | NPS, CSAT, support ticket trends |
| Risk Signals | 10% | Key contact departure, usage decline, competitor search activity |

## Product Roadmap Methodology (Now-Next-Later)

### Now-Next-Later Three-Horizon Roadmap

```
Now (Current Quarter)     Next (Next 1-2 Quarters)     Later (Future)
─────────────────    ──────────────────    ──────────────────
Definitely doing          High priority, pending planning     Directional exploration
Clear delivery timeline   Resources reserved                 No fixed timeline
                          Requirements validated             Requirements pending validation
```

### Outcome-Based Roadmap

Traditional Roadmap: Q1 build Feature A → Q2 build Feature B (Output-oriented)
Outcome Roadmap: Q1 target increase activation rate 30% → Q2 target reduce churn 20% (Outcome-oriented)

### Roadmap Communication Tips

| Audience | Focus | Presentation Style |
|----------|-------|-------------------|
| **Executives / Board** | Strategic direction, ROI | Now-Next-Later + Key metrics |
| **Sales / Customer Success** | When will what be delivered | Timeline + Feature list |
| **Engineering Team** | Technical dependencies, resource needs | Sprint-level detailed plan |
| **Customers** | When will their requests be done | "Doing / Planned / Considering" three categories |
